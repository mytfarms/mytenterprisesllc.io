Our Story

Who We Are

MYT Enterprises, LLC is a multi-faceted operation rooted in hard work, integrity, and a deep respect for the land and the people we serve. We run three divisions — auctions, livestock, and construction — each one grounded in the same principles that have guided this operation from the start.

We've built our reputation one handshake at a time. Not through flashy advertising or empty promises, but by showing up, doing the work, and standing behind what we do. In the auction ring, that means transparent, expertly run events that serve buyers and sellers equally. On the ranch, it means raising quality livestock with care and attention. On the job site, it means solid builds that last.

This is a family operation. We know what it means to work long days and longer seasons, to take pride in what we build, and to treat the people we work with the way we'd want to be treated ourselves. That's not a mission statement — it's just how we were raised. Cade Christensen

What Drives Us

How We Work

Integrity First

We say what we mean and do what we say. Whether it's representing an animal at sale, pricing a build, or calling an auction — honesty isn't negotiable.

Hands-On, Always

This isn't a business run from a desk. We're on the ground — in the pen, on the site, behind the mic. You'll work with the people who are actually doing the work.

Respect for the Land

Colorado's land and livestock heritage matters to us. We operate with a long view — taking care of the ground, the animals, and the community that depends on both.

Built to Last

Whether it's a pole barn, a corral, or a relationship with a buyer — we build things that hold up. Short cuts cost more in the long run, and we'd rather do it right the first time.

Certified Auctioneer

Auction School & What Came Next

In March 2025, we sent a member of our team to the World Wide College of Auctioneering in Des Moines, Iowa — one of the most respected auction schools in the country. Eight days of intensive bid calling, business education, legal foundations, and real-world instruction from industry leaders.

The experience didn't just produce a certified auctioneer. It sharpened the business side of how we operate — from marketing and consignment to the mechanics of running a professional auction event. We came back with new tools, new contacts, and a higher standard to hold ourselves to.

That investment is now part of everything we do in the auction division. Read the full account of that experience on the Auctions page.
